FOR PARENTS

Clear Expectations.
Structured Learning.
Real Progress.

I believe successful learning comes from a partnership between student, parent and coach. This page explains exactly how I teach, communicate and support every student's progress.

MY APPROACH

Quality Over Quantity

Every lesson is individually planned around the student's current level, learning style and long-term goals. Rather than rushing through worksheets, I focus on understanding, critical thinking and building confidence that lasts beyond examinations.

TEACHING PHILOSOPHY

What Every Student Can Expect

Understand Before Memorising

Students learn why concepts work before applying them independently.

Personalised Coaching

Lessons adapt to every student's pace rather than following identical lesson plans.

Long-Term Growth

The goal is independent learners, not temporary exam improvements.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION

Working Together

Homework

+

Homework is an essential part of the learning process rather than optional extra practice. Every assignment is designed to reinforce concepts introduced during lessons and identify areas that require further attention. Students are expected to complete homework before the next lesson. Incomplete work limits lesson efficiency, as valuable teaching time must be spent reviewing unfinished tasks instead of introducing new concepts.

Lesson Preparation

+

Students should arrive on time with all required learning materials, completed homework and a stable internet connection. Being prepared allows every lesson to begin immediately and ensures that valuable lesson time is focused on learning rather than setup or troubleshooting. A quiet learning environment with minimal distractions is strongly recommended for younger students.

Parent Communication

+

Parents are encouraged to maintain open communication regarding their child's progress, concerns or academic goals. Questions may be discussed outside lesson hours through Messenger, Zalo or email. While I strive to respond promptly, replies may occasionally take longer during teaching hours.

Progress Reviews

+

Learning progress is monitored continuously rather than only through examination results. Parents receive honest feedback regarding strengths, weaknesses and areas that require additional practice. As students develop, lesson plans are adjusted to ensure steady, long-term improvement instead of short-term memorisation.

Attendance

+

Consistent attendance is one of the most important factors in successful language learning. Missing lessons frequently interrupts learning momentum and slows long-term progress. Families are encouraged to inform me as early as possible if a lesson needs to be rescheduled.

Questions Outside Lessons

+

Students are welcome to ask short questions between lessons whenever clarification is needed. This helps prevent misunderstandings from developing into larger learning gaps before the next session. More complex topics that require detailed explanation will usually be addressed during the following lesson.
