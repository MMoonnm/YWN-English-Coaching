ABOUT ME

More Than Two Decades Learning English. Three Years Coaching It.

I was born to a Korean and Vietnamese family and grew up in an international education environment from kindergarten onwards. For more than twenty years, English has been the primary language used throughout my education, learning under teachers from different English-speaking countries while following an international curriculum.

This international upbringing allowed me to experience English naturally—not simply as a school subject, but as the language used every day for learning, communication and academic growth.

For the past three years, I have specialised in personalised online coaching for Cambridge English, IELTS and the Digital SAT. Every lesson is individually planned around each student's strengths, weaknesses and long-term goals rather than relying on a fixed textbook or one-size-fits-all approach.

My objective extends beyond examination results. I aim to help students become independent English users who understand the language, think critically and communicate confidently in real academic and professional environments.

MY PHILOSOPHY

English Is A Language,
Not A Formula.

Many students spend years memorising grammar rules but still struggle to communicate naturally. My goal isn't simply to help students remember English. It's to help them think in English.

Think Before Translating

Beginners naturally rely on Vietnamese while learning English, and that's completely normal. As students become more confident, I gradually encourage them to think directly in English instead of translating every sentence word by word. This develops faster reading, listening and speaking skills.

Communication Before Memorisation

Memorising model answers may help in one situation, but genuine communication requires flexibility. Rather than memorising conversations, students learn how English works so they can confidently express their own ideas.

Learn From Mistakes

Mistakes are one of the most valuable parts of learning. Students are encouraged to speak and write freely while I identify recurring errors, explain why they happen and provide strategies to prevent them from becoming habits.

Build Independent Learners

Success isn't measured by how long students study with me. It's measured by how confidently they can eventually learn, analyse and communicate independently.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Every Lesson Has A Purpose

01

Assessment

Every student begins with an assessment to identify their current level, strengths, weaknesses and learning goals. Lessons are then planned around those findings rather than following a generic syllabus.

02

Structured Lessons

Every lesson has clear objectives. Students always know what they are learning, why it matters and how it connects to previous lessons.

03

Continuous Feedback

Students receive corrections throughout lessons instead of waiting until the end. This encourages immediate improvement and helps prevent repeated mistakes.

04

Long-Term Growth

The objective is never short-term memorisation. Lessons are designed to develop confidence, independence and lifelong English skills that continue beyond examinations.
